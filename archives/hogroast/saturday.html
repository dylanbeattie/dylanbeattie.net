Thee Jubilee Hogge-Roaste

Episode 1: Saturday

(in which our intrepid heroes build a bar, dig a pit, buy a fridge, get drunk and pass out.)

There's anticipation in the streets, excitement in the air and a pig in the fridge... yes, you join us for the third annual hog-roast, on a bright clear Jubilee sunday morning. There's delightful sense of the theatrical about walking into the butchers of a sunday morning and paying a small fortune for a large dead thing wrapped in black bin-liners... like they've cleaned and gutted your ex-wife for you or something. :)

In fact, considering the level of last night's festivities, it's astonishing anyone's even alive... I vaguely remember eating biltong and drinking Becherovka at about three this morning, and I distinctly recall that soul-destroying moment when you finally decide to go to bed, walk out of the living room and realise the sun's rising, the birds are singing and your body clock is about to get fucked big-style. Everyone seems to have escaped rather lightly this time around.... there's movement and consciousness from all quarters, which can only be a Good Thing.

I take it as a good omen that for the first time, the weather is sufficiently clement that we're actually having to refrigerate the hog... this did, it must be said, involve throwing away quite a lot of food, but most of it went off last year anyway so that's probably another Good Thing.

This time around, we were fortunate enough to have the services of a Qualified Digger of Pits and Holes, who was also fool enough to dig the entire pit by himself. Well done that man. And what a splendid pit, if I might say so.

There's this unwritten law that every party has to exceed it's predecessor by an order of magnitude, in size, scale, ambition and just plain stupidity. You see, if someone goes to two parties and they're the same, then they'll assume they know what to expect of the next one... and where's the fun in that? We've done cocktails, fancy dress, demolition of various forms, pyrotechnics, fireworks, fire-breathing... but throughout all these endeavours, there's always been this damned inconvenience of having to go to the fridge whenever a beverage is required.

And so it came to pass that, sitting outside one spring evening with very little to do, the voices in my head suggest putting a bar on the site of the long-deceased shed... (which is another party story for another time). One internet slippage later, and we've got a rack of optics. A bit of improvisation, a bit of carpentry and quite a lot of drilling later, and we've got something approaching a bar. With nothing behind it. And then - surely a sign of divine endorsement if ever such a thing existed - we head out to procure 'bar supplies' and they're selling fridges. Little tiny branded fridges, with Stella Artois written on them. And instructions in the manual that 'this fridge is not to be used for food or hot beverages'. So naturally, we did what any self-respecting drinker would do in the circumstances. We - or rather that nice Mister Visa person - bought a fridge. You can see it here, happily chilling beers and purring quietly to itself.



Of course, having built the bar, it was necessary to test it - unleashing an untested bar onto our unsuspecting guests would be the last thing we'd want to do. After that, everything went a little bit hazy.

Rearrange these pictures so they tell a story. Go on. 'Cos we have no idea what the fuck was going on... although occasional nuggets of recollection drift to the surface, like the eyeballs in an ethnic soup... some truly outstanding bartending (although not by my bro, who sat there 'hard selling' people green drinks until he had to be physically removed), some truly extraordinary cocktails (including one which managed to go from red to blue without becoming purple at any point along the way), the invention of the 'Warhol' (above centre), the summoning of the Beer Goblin (top right), the cocktail mixer from hell (bottom left), some rather pointless games with foil, and my deciding that the bar would be a much better place to sleep than my bed, which was a good fifty feet away at this point...

And that's just the warm-up. Watch this space :)
